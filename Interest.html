Painting
Painting is one of the oldest and most widespread art forms. Originally, it was used as a form of communication between people. Today, painting continues to be used for its aesthetic value, primarily as a means of expression. Painting can be used to evoke different emotions in people, and has been used throughout history to express political messages and criticize social institutions.
Painting like other art forms can evoke emotions, make people think and even instill a sense of pride. Artists record history or tell a story and sometimes both. There is the painting of General George Washington crossing the Delaware River in winter. It is a classic painting that made many people see into an artist's mind what his impression was of that event. Artists and other thinkers are always the first ones the authorities seek to remove when they are threatened with descent or called upon when they want to convey an idea.
